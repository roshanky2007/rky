Food Preservation by Freezing

Learn how freezing helps extend food shelf life while maintaining safety and nutrition.

How Freezing Preserves Food

  • ❄️ Low temperatures inhibit bacteria, yeasts, and molds.
  • ❄️ Water inside food forms ice crystals, preventing microbial activity.
  • ❄️ Nutritional value, taste, and texture are preserved with proper storage.

Types of Freezing

Slow Freezing (Conventional Freezing): Forms large ice crystals, may damage texture.

Fast Freezing (Flash Freezing): Forms small ice crystals, better texture retention.

Cryogenic Freezing: Uses liquid nitrogen or CO₂ for ultra-fast freezing.

Steps for Freezing Food

Preparation: Wash, peel, and cut food into portions. Blanch some vegetables to maintain quality.

Packaging: Use airtight containers or vacuum-sealed bags. Label with the date.

Freezing: Set freezer to at least -18°C (0°F) for optimal preservation.

Storage & Thawing: Store properly to avoid freezer burn. Thaw safely in the refrigerator.

Benefits of Freezing

  • ✅ Retains nutrients, color, and flavor.
  • ✅ Extends shelf life (months to years).
  • ✅ Reduces food waste.
  • ✅ No need for preservatives.
