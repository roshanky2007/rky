What is Food Preservation?

Food preservation is the process of treating and handling food to stop or slow down spoilage, loss of quality, and contamination. It ensures that food remains safe, edible, and nutritious for an extended period.

Why is Food Preservation Important?

Food preservation plays a crucial role in reducing waste, maintaining nutrition, and ensuring food security.

✅ Reduces food waste by extending shelf life.
✅ Maintains nutritional value and taste.
✅ Ensures food availability during off-seasons.
✅ Prevents the growth of harmful bacteria and mold.
✅ Supports food security and sustainability.

Food Preservation Techniques

Canning

Lock in freshness with heat-sealed jars for long-term storage.

Fermentation

Enhance flavors naturally using beneficial bacteria.

Dehydration

Remove moisture to keep food fresh without refrigeration.

Freezing

Store food at low temperatures to retain taste and texture.

Salting

Use salt to naturally extend the shelf life of perishable foods.
