Food Preservation by Dehydration

Learn how dehydration helps extend food shelf life while maintaining safety and nutrition.

What is Dehydration?

Dehydration is a preservation method that removes moisture from food to prevent spoilage. By reducing water content, enzymes and bacteria cannot thrive, allowing food to be stored for long periods without refrigeration.

Methods of Dehydration

Sun Drying: Uses natural sunlight and airflow to dry food.

Air Drying: Similar to sun drying but done in a shaded, well-ventilated area.

Oven Drying: Uses low heat (50–70°C) to dry food.

Dehydrator Drying: Uses a specialized appliance with controlled temperature and airflow.

Freeze Drying: Food is frozen and then placed in a vacuum, where ice sublimates into vapor.

Steps in Dehydration

  • Wash, peel, and slice food into thin, even pieces.
  • Arrange food in a single layer on drying trays.
  • Dry using the chosen method until food reaches the desired texture.
  • Let dried food cool completely before storing.

Benefits of Dehydration

  • Extends shelf life for months to years.
  • Preserves nutrients and intensifies flavor.
  • Reduces food weight and space for easy storage.
  • Prevents spoilage without preservatives.
