Food Preservation by Canning

Learn how canning helps extend food shelf life while maintaining safety and nutrition.

What is Canning?

Canning is a preservation method that seals food in airtight containers to prevent spoilage. It kills microorganisms and prevents contamination.

Types of Canning

Water Bath Canning: Ideal for high-acid foods like fruits, jams, and pickles.

Pressure Canning: Used for low-acid foods like meats, vegetables, and soups.

Steps in Canning

  • Filling Jars: Pack food into sterilized jars.
  • Sealing: Wipe rims and secure lids properly.
  • Processing: Use a boiling water bath or pressure canner.
  • Cooling & Storage: Allow jars to cool before storing.

Benefits of Canning

  • Preserves food for months or years.
  • Maintains taste and nutritional value.
  • Reduces food waste.
  • Provides additive-free homemade food options.
