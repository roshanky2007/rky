Food Preservation by Salting

Learn how salting helps preserve food by drawing out moisture and preventing microbial growth.

Types of Salting

Dry Salting (Curing): Salt is directly applied to food. Used for meat, fish, and vegetables.

Brining (Wet Salting): Food is submerged in a saltwater solution. Used for pickles, olives, cheese, and meats.

Steps in Salting

Preparation: Clean and cut food into sizes. Choose dry salting or brining.

Applying Salt: Dry salting: Coat food evenly with salt. Brining: Dissolve salt in water and soak food.

Storage & Maturation: Keep salted food in airtight containers. Store in a cool place or refrigerate.

Examples of Salt-Preserved Foods

  • Meat: Bacon, jerky, salted beef.
  • Fish: Salted cod, anchovies.
  • Dairy: Feta cheese, halloumi.
  • Vegetables: Sauerkraut, pickles.

Benefits of Salting

  • ✅ Prevents bacterial growth and spoilage.
  • ✅ Extends food shelf life.
  • ✅ Enhances flavor and texture.
  • ✅ Simple and cost-effective.
